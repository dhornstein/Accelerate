New Zealand

As you may or may not know, I'm from the tiny island country of New Zealand. Us Kiwis are a very proud nation of around 4.5 million people -- so that's approximately half the size of Hong Kong. One of our oldest traditions which stemmed from the ancient Maori tribes is a "haka" or in English it's commonly known as a war dance. The haka is primarily used as both a greeting and to set a challenge to an opposition. The below inset video is a haka performed by the All Blacks who are New Zealand's top rugby team. This particular haka in the video is called "Kapa O Pango" and it is a relatively new haka only being performed for the first time in 2005 whereas the original haka "Ka Mate" has been performed since 1820.



To give you an idea of my hometown, Christchurch linked below is a city of around 450,000 people. Yes, tiny! I think LKF has more people in the bars on a Friday and Saturday night than the entire population of my home town. Christchurch is situated in the provivence of Canterbury which is the largest province in the South Island of New Zealand.
